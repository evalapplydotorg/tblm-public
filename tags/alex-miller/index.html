This Place can be Beautiful Again

Alex Miller
06 Jun 2013 / Issue 1 / fiction

I sat by the river, on a damp log half-buried in mud, and watched how the yellow finger of water wound its way through the city, past gutters and factories and underneath bridges, and it smelled like the worst thing in the world.
