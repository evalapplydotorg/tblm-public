Deepti Kapoor – interviewed by Michelle D’Costa

May 7
This interview was conducted by e-mail. It is about Deepti Kapoor’s novel A Bad Character, first published by Penguin Random House, 2014. About the novel (from Goodreads) A highly charged fiction debut about a young woman in India, and the love that both shatters and transforms her. She is twenty, restless in New Delhi. Her mother has died; her father has left for Singapore. He is a few years older, just back to India from New York.

Two Poems – by Suhit Kelkar

May 7
Isosceles Majesty It fluttered into sight, forming ripples on the night’s cool surface – a faint beacon of grey, the outlines blending. It settled on the doorframe, within reach of my tiny palms, and I caught a glimpse of a plump body tapering to a pointy end. It would have been merely incongruous without the isosceles majesty of swept-back wings – stiff, powder-coated, frantic, flapping in my fumbling grasp. I grabbed the strange creature.
