Two poems

Kuhu Joshi
10 Oct 2020 / Issue D8 / poetry

The day of the fitting I enter the basement of the Spinal Injuries Hospital. It is white, walls and a bed and a curtain. Mom is holding my hand. There is a doctor, also in white. He does not look at me. He says namaste to Mom. He dips his hand into a tub of white liquid, pouring more white powder into it. She will have to take her clothes off, he says while mixing.
