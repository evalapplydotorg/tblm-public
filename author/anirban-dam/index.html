Three poems

Anirban Dam
10 Oct 2021 / Issue E5 / poetry

Turmeric My mother is a shade of cinnamon, probably less brittle. She wears bangles — white, red — replaces them every five years. They have traces of gold in them, and grief. You can see them quiver when she kneads, her wrists are hollow flutes albeit clogged by dough and domesticity. Veins, like inlets, coursing through her arm converging at some point in her body where everything turbulent settles down.

Two Poems

Anirban Dam
03 Mar 2020 / Issue D4 / poetry

Erosion (To preserve the format of the poem, we have made it available in a PDF document. Please click here to access.) Eviction And so it begins, the slow and weary process of eviction. Names, faces, sounds, facades, all stacked up like souvenirs waiting for their turn, hoping to survive another cycle. Time and again, this table has witnessed the unbecoming of a story, or a moment becoming untraceable—you leaning against the wall, exhaling portions of nostalgia as the window tries to fathom every possible shape of departure.
