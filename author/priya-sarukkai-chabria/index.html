The Mud Fort of Khiva
Priya Sarukkai Chabria
May 25, 2021 / Issue 52 / poetry (~1 min. read)
Crenulated by ancient toil this wall of sand stands against the sky and stretches. At night it turns homely: restaurants light up its alleys. We get drunk on its shrunken size and our escape from the guide’s intoning of its history. Especially the slave chambers, ferret holes in which they were shoved, stacked and sold – on which Khiva thrived for centuries. The cages aren’t empty but thick with pleading eyes
