Fish Eyes

Michelle D’costa
10 Oct 2018 / Issue / poetry

_For Mama _ *** Mom and I talk in the kitchen only. I follow her and say, in English, I miss the Arab fisherman from Marina beach terribly. The joy in your eyes when he caught a fish every Saturday was special. She says in Konkani. Dole pole. She points at the fish eyes. I search for solidarity. Stale. Should have gone early to the market, she sighs, her finger still pointing.

The Help

Michelle D’Costa
04 Apr 2014 / Issue / fiction

She aroused interest in Bradley like a sealed envelope could do. He was dumb but not deaf and she did not know that. When she realised he was dumb she assumed that he had to be deaf too. She was the help but she was not hired to look after him. Little did she know that to him the sounds of her washing the dishes were a melody of their own.
