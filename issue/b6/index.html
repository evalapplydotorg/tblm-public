At the Cafés of Your Memory

Huzaifa Pandit
02 Feb 2018 / Issue B6 / poetry

Faiz, what befell the fellow travelers of late last night? Where did the morning breeze halt? Where did morning alight? My nearly dead phone flares up with a call from home: My mother laments in frayed Kashmiri: _I am happy you aren’t home, two boys were shot dead today.

The Birth

Anushka Joshi
02 Feb 2018 / Issue B6 / fiction

It was February when we wheeled my sister into the hospital, her eyes shut close with pain. There was always someone touching her hair, her hand, always someone telling her that it would be alright, everything would be fine, but also sobbing as they said it, while she drifted in and out of consciousness. The hospital was quiet, the nurses talked quietly. People waiting outside the ICU wore sweaters and caps in bright colors.
