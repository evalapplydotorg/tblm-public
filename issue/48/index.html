Kuhu Joshi
October 28, 2020 / Issue 48 / poetry (~2 min. read)
The day of the fitting I enter the basement of the Spinal Injuries Hospital. It is white, walls and a bed and a curtain. Mom is holding my hand. There is a doctor, also in white. He does not look at me. He says namaste to Mom. He dips his hand into a tub of white liquid, pouring more white powder into it. She will have to take her clothes off, he says while mixing.

Three poems
Beni Yanthan
October 28, 2020 / Issue 48 / poetry (~1 min. read)
Delicious Vowels This is how you sound. Like strips of indecipherable syllables floating inside a lullaby. This is how you taste. Like the first breath of air a drowning man sips when he is finally free of the echo of the sea. This is how you move. Like spoken-word poems that carry the finality of the written word in their voices. Break, break, break. It wasn’t meant to be.

Postmortem
Devanshi Khetarpal
October 28, 2020 / Issue 48 / poetry (~1 min. read)
Postmortem Listen: imagine your body next to mine. Think of the knife that drew my thin spine open. I am in a room so cold and lonely like the slow negative of a stone breaking into water. One evening I asked you how to die this slow: smooth, bent along the edges of my grave. Almost losing my way back home. ***
