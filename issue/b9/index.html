Joan of Mazgaon

Zenisha Gonsalves
06 Jun 2018 / Issue B9 / fiction

Every morning Joan and Gilly wake up before their children do, set the house and day in order, settle down in the balcony with two cups of tea and a newspaper. Joan cuts a loaf of bread and a pair of tomatoes – do tomatoes come in pairs? she wonders – takes the chutney from the big fridge in the small kitchen and places it all on the table where they eat.

Another Earth

Amrendra Pandey
06 Jun 2018 / Issue B9 / poetry

Distant memories approach me – I am looking in a water well, an elephant is plucking peepul leaves in the dense forest, and her mahout is laughing. I am sprinting along narrow past-roads, fields of corns and pulses covered in evening smoke, the villages of many doors and windows. It was my fifth grade when I saw a snake slithering in the harvested wheat field. A bare tree and its succulent drumsticks.
